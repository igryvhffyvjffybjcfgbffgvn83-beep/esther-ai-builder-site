BA Process Evidence

From scattered pain points to controlled operational change

This section explains the analysis path behind the demo: stakeholder needs, problem framing, stage-gate governance, process mapping, UAT readiness and benefit validation.

1 · Identify

Problem intake

Capture ideas from email, meetings and Slack into a single triage queue with a one-page problem statement.

2 · Analyse

Current state analysis

Interview stakeholders, confirm root cause, map As-Is handoffs and quantify operational delay.

3 · Design

To-Be solution design

Define business rules, exception handling, controls and acceptance criteria before implementation.

4 · Implement

UAT and rollout

Prepare test scenarios, confirm sign-off and document training or change-management needs.

5 · Review

Benefits realisation

Measure actual hours saved, capture lessons learned and identify reusable improvement patterns.

Traceability Thread

Stakeholder concern

Regional managers manually assign new client cases and miss CRM updates.

Requirement

System must support rules-based assignment using workload, expertise and location.

Design control

Assignment reason is logged and exception cases route to manual review.

Measured outcome

Average allocation time reduced from 4.2 hours to approximately 5 minutes.

Methodology

BA governance model used in the demo

The demo combines stage-gate governance, impact-effort scoring, process analysis, controlled delivery and PIR-based benefit validation.

Framework Components

Stage-gate workflow

Each initiative must pass an explicit decision point before moving to the next delivery stage.

Impact / effort scoring

Requests are prioritised by business impact, frequency, compliance risk, staff pain, effort and data readiness.

Benefits realisation

Target savings and actual savings are tracked separately to avoid overstating projected value.
